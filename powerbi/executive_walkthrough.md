# RetailPulse Executive Walkthrough

## Recommended Boardroom Flow

### 1. Open With The Executive Dashboard
- Start with the KPI strip so the room gets the current state in one glance.
- Call out revenue, profit, churn, forecast quality, and inventory savings first.
- Use the trend line and map to show whether the result is broad-based or concentrated in one market.

### 2. Confirm The Sales Story
- Move to `Sales Analytics` to explain what is driving revenue.
- Point to growth pockets, margin pressure, and basket quality.
- Use the product ranking to separate high performers from noise.

### 3. Validate The Customer Base
- Open `Customer Intelligence` to show which segments are most valuable.
- Identify champion customers, loyal groups, and accounts that need attention.
- Highlight whether CLV is concentrated in a small share of the base.

### 4. Review Forecast Confidence
- Switch to `Demand Forecasting` and confirm that the planning view is stable.
- Show the best model and the main error metrics.
- Use the forecast band to explain where the plan is dependable and where manual review is needed.

### 5. Check Inventory Readiness
- Move to `Inventory Intelligence` to show whether stock is aligned with demand.
- Focus on reorder urgency, safety stock, and stock coverage.
- Explain where savings come from and where service risk remains high.

### 6. Address Retention Risk
- Open `Customer Churn` to identify the customers that matter most.
- Call out the critical and high-risk cohorts first.
- Tie the recommended action to the likely business outcome.

### 7. Inspect Product And Geography Concentration
- Use `Product Analytics` to show which products carry the business.
- Use `Geographic Analytics` to show which markets are driving concentration or risk.
- Keep the story focused on action, not just reporting.

### 8. Close With The Executive Summary
- Finish with the summary page so the room leaves with a short decision list.
- State the top three actions: protect revenue, protect service, and protect retention.
- Use the board-pack bookmarks to switch between executive, finance, and operations views if needed.

## Meeting Outcome
- The leadership team should leave with a clear view of performance, risk, and next actions.
- The report should support fast decisions without requiring a separate analyst to interpret every chart.
